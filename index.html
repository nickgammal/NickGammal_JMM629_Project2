Northeast Pacific Coast Food Web

The California Current System is characterized by cold water, deep bathymetry, and seasonal upwelling. The upwelling events make this coastline one of the most productive in the world. Kelp forests are one type of ecosystem that exists within this current. They are vast fields of kelp that can reach as tall as 60 meters, providing not just nourishment, but shelter and structure for tens of thousands of other species.

This network displays a food web of a kelp forest ecosystem at the phylogenetic class level. For example, the dark red bubble "Mammalia" represents all the marine mammals in this kelp forest: whales, dolphins, otters, orcas, seals, sea lions, and more. The lines connecting the bubbles represent consumption, where one organism consumes the other.

The ecosystem is balanced, because each group of organisms relies on the ones below it on the food chain to survive. In some cases, the existence of the entire system can rely on a specific species to keep the balance. These are called "keystone species". Right-click on the bubbles to remove them and find out which two of these groups contain keystone species.
